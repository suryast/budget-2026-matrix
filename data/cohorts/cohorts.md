These tags describe political salience, not exclusive audiences. Median cell should carry a short set of tags that explain why the action becomes politically loud in that part of the electorate.

## Demographic
- `gen_z`: Gen Z — Born approximately 1997 to 2012.
- `millennial`: Millennial — Born approximately 1981 to 1996.
- `gen_x`: Gen X — Born approximately 1965 to 1980.
- `boomer`: Boomer — Born approximately 1946 to 1964.
- `pensioner`: Pensioner — Income-support recipient, including age pension or DSP.

## Economic
- `renter`: Renter — Rents primary residence.
- `fhb_aspirant`: First-home buyer aspirant — Saving toward a first-home deposit.
- `owner_occupier`: Owner-occupier — Owns primary residence and reads policy through household cash flow.
- `landlord`: Landlord — Holds investment property and cares about deduction treatment.
- `asset_rich_retiree`: Asset-rich retiree — Material non-super assets and drawdown exposure.

## Electoral
- `inner_metro_progressive`: Inner-metro progressive — Inner-city Labor or Greens seats with large professional class exposure.
- `outer_suburb_mortgage_belt`: Outer-suburb mortgage belt — Marginal outer-suburb seats shaped by mortgage stress and household cash flow.
- `regional`: Regional — Non-metropolitan seats with different business and housing exposures.
- `teal_seat`: Teal seat — Independent-held former Liberal seats with high professional investor exposure.
- `safe_coalition`: Safe Coalition — Safe Liberal or Nationals seats where landlord and small-business narratives travel well.
